Seyi Olojo

I'm a PhD student at UC Berkeley School of Information where I am advised by Dr. Jenna Burrell. My research lies within the intersection of critical data studies and critical race theory (CRT). I primarily explore the social construction of numbers and the social contexts in which they are used. I specifically explore these themes within two research areas. First, I explore how we legitimize and understand public sector data. For example, through the census. Secondly, I study early stage algorthim development. I'm primarily concerned with issues of data acquisition and quality. I utlize black feminist epistimologies to understand the social lives of numbers.

I recieved my bachelor's degree in Environmental Policy at Barnard College where I was a Mellon Mays Undergraduate Scholar. After college, I worked in Ad Tech as a data analyst.
