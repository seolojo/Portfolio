Seyi Olojo

I'm a PhD student at the UC Berkeley School of Information where I am advised by Dr. Jenna Burrell. My research interests are in AI ethics, the sociology of quantification and epistemologies of categories within emerging technologies. My research primarily explores the visibility politics of information, specifically emphasizing the differential representation of contested identities within demographic categories and datasets. I use critical race theory and black feminist epistemology to apply a critical lens in understanding the implications of technology on society. At Berkeley, I'm a member of The Algorithmic Fairness and Opacity Group and have been awarded the Eugene Cota-Robles Fellowship to support my research. My earlier work investigated the mechanics and robustness of proxy development in indices used for climate vulnerability analyses.

Prior to graduate school I worked as a data analyst for an advertising technology company based in New York City. I also earned my bachelor’s degree in Environmental Policy at Barnard College. When I’m not thinking about research, I moonlight as a recipe developer. I'm currently sheltering in place in the East Coast with my family and my beloved pup, Juno.
